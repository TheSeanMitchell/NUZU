Privacy Policy

Effective Date: April 6, 2026  |  Last Updated: April 6, 2026

Short version: NUZU News does not sell your data, does not serve tracking cookies, and does not collect personally identifiable information unless you voluntarily submit it (e.g., via our newsletter form). We use browser local storage only to save your in-app preferences.

1. About NUZU News

NUZU News ("NUZU," "we," "us," or "our") is a news aggregation service that curates publicly available headlines from third-party news publishers. NUZU does not publish original content. All linked articles remain the property of their respective publishers.

2. Information We Collect

We collect only the minimum information necessary to provide our service:

  • Device Local Storage: We store your preferences (light/dark mode, section collapse state, scroll position, read articles) locally on your device using the browser's localStorage API. This data never leaves your device and is not transmitted to our servers.
  • Newsletter Submissions: If you voluntarily submit your email address via our newsletter signup form, that email is processed by Formspree (a GDPR-compliant third-party form service) and delivered to our inbox. We do not sell, rent, or share your email address with any third party.
  • Analytics (Google Analytics 4): We use Google Analytics 4 to understand aggregate traffic patterns (page views, session duration, geographic region). This data is anonymized and does not personally identify you. You may opt out via the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.
  • Error Logs: Our hosting provider (GitHub Pages) may log server-side access data (IP address, browser type, requested URL) as part of standard web server operation. We do not control or access these logs for any purpose.

3. Information We Do NOT Collect

  • We do not use advertising tracking cookies.
  • We do not build user profiles or behavioral fingerprints.
  • We do not collect your name, address, phone number, or payment information.
  • We do not use cross-site trackers, pixel tags, or beacon scripts.
  • We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with data brokers.

4. Third-Party Services

NUZU embeds or links to the following third-party services, each governed by their own privacy policy:

  • YouTube (Google LLC): Live video feeds embedded on our page. YouTube may set cookies when videos are loaded. See Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Google Analytics 4 (Google LLC): Aggregate traffic analytics. See Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Formspree: Newsletter/contact form processing. See Formspree's Privacy Policy.
  • GitHub Pages (Microsoft): Website hosting. See GitHub's Privacy Statement.
  • News Publishers: All outbound links to articles are governed by the respective publisher's privacy policy. NUZU is not responsible for third-party content or privacy practices.

5. Cookies

NUZU's own code does not set any cookies. The browser's localStorage API (which we use for preferences) is not a cookie and does not transmit data to servers. Third-party embeds (YouTube) may set their own cookies as described in Section 4.

6. Children's Privacy

NUZU News is intended for users aged 13 and older. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has submitted personal information to us, please contact us immediately at the address below and we will delete it promptly.

7. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights:

  • Access & Deletion: Since we store your preferences only locally on your device, you can delete all NUZU data at any time by clearing your browser's local storage or app data.
  • Email Opt-Out: If you subscribed to our newsletter, you may unsubscribe at any time by replying to any newsletter email with "UNSUBSCRIBE" in the subject line.
  • California (CCPA): California residents have the right to know what personal information is collected, request deletion, and opt out of sale. NUZU does not sell personal information. To exercise rights, contact us below.
  • EU/EEA (GDPR): EU residents have rights of access, rectification, erasure, portability, and objection. Our legal basis for analytics processing is legitimate interest. To exercise rights, contact us below.

8. Data Security

All data transmitted to and from NUZU News is encrypted via HTTPS (TLS). We do not store sensitive personal data on our servers. Your preference data is stored exclusively on your own device.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last Updated" date at the top of this page will reflect any changes. Continued use of NUZU News after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
